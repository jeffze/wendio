WENDIO

Learning numbers in the language — by playing, together.
Deer · Wolf · Bear · Turtle

1A language revitalization tool

Wendio is not just a game: it is a language revitalization tool designed for First Nations language programs.

Each number is presented in three layers — the numeral, the word in your language, and a phonetic guide — so that young and old learn by playing, in a real group atmosphere. A bingo-style game, easy to host, where the language is at the centre.

2What Wendio brings you

🎲

A group activity

A host draws the numbers; participants play on their phone or tablet — or on printable paper cards.

🔊

The voice of your Elders

An authentic audio recording for every number, in your language, built into the game.

🪶

In your nation's image

Vocabulary, clans, symbols and colours customized for your community.

📱

Web + app

Nothing to install in the browser, plus an Android app. On computer, tablet or phone.

🛡️

Turnkey

Hosting, updates and support included. Simple login, no password to remember.

🤝

Built with First Nations

Created hand in hand with the Wendat, its founding nation.

3Funding Wendio: an expense already covered

Your nation does not have to draw on its own funds. Public programs fund exactly this kind of Indigenous language revitalization tool:

  • Indigenous Languages Program — federal (Canadian Heritage, under the Indigenous Languages Act). In Québec, access is through the Regional Committee on Ancestral Languages (CRLA); Wendio falls under the “project stream”.
  • Indigenous Cultural Partnership — Québec, with language vitality as a priority.

We help you include Wendio in your language plan. Programs and their deadlines change every year — outside Québec, each region has its equivalent body.

Frequently asked questions
Can we try before committing?
Yes. The Discovery package is free: you test Wendio with a real group before any decision.
Is it customized to our language?
Yes. The vocabulary, the audio (the voice of your Elders), your clans and symbols, and your branding are built into your version.
Does it work in a room, with a group?
Yes. A host leads, participants play on their devices — and you can print paper cards to play off-screen.
On which devices?
In a web browser (nothing to install) and via an Android app. Computer, tablet or phone.
What about support, updates, our data?
Hosting, updates and support are included. Your recordings and your content remain your property.
How is Wendio funded?
Through Indigenous language programs (see “Funding Wendio”). We help you include it in your language plan.
So how much does it cost, exactly?
Installation is about $2,000 (one time), then an annual subscription: free with Discovery, ~$750 with Community, $2,400 to $3,600 for the Nation package depending on your funding.
